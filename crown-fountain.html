Crown Fountain

Millennium Park, Chicago

About Crown Fountain

Crown Fountain, also known as the "Squirting Faces" is an interactive public art installation designed by Spanish artist named Jaume Plensa, it opened in July 2004. It features two 50-foot glass block towers that project video images of the Chicago residents faces.

What makes it special:The water flows from the towers and appears to spout from the mouths of the projected faces that makes a playful and surprising effect. It is a popular subject for photographers and a common gathering place.

Fun Fact:Over 1,000 Chicago residents were filmed for the video display, representing the city's diversity.
